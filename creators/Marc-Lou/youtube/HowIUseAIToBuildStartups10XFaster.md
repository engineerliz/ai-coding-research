## Summary

Marc Lou walks through the AI stack he uses to spin up SaaS experiments: Cursor for inline instructions, plan-mode chat, Composer, and the design surface; MCP-powered browser hooks so Cursor can ingest console and network logs; and prefab codebases like ShipFast or Lovable to keep context consistent. He argues that naming conventions, reusable boilerplates, and clearly scoped prompts give Cursor enough grounding to rewrite back-end, front-end, and design layers in minutes while he stays in reviewer mode. The second half shows him delegating multi-file edits to Composer, piping prototypes into Lovable for UI polish, and using the 80/20 rule (AI drafts, human QA) to keep velocity without skipping manual review. He closes by urging solo founders to ship the marketing site, data model, and onboarding flow in one Cursor session so they can validate demand before overbuilding.
